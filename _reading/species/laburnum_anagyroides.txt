A small tree, much planted in parks, gardens and on waysides, thriving mainly on acid soils and frequently self-seeding into waste ground and by roads and railways. In W. Britain it was formerly used in hedging, and persists locally. Generally lowland, but reaching 425 m near New Radnor (Rads.).
